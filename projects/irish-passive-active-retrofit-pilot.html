Purpose

Why the pilot was developed

Irish retrofit decisions are often framed around BER improvement, fabric upgrades and heat-pump readiness. This pilot shows how passive envelope measures and heat-pump design variables can be assessed together so that cost, emissions, comfort risk and heat-pump operating performance are visible in the same decision.

The project is intentionally a proof-of-work rather than a national-stock model. It demonstrates the methodological readiness behind an Ireland-focused decision-support workflow that can later be scaled to multiple Irish dwelling archetypes and validated through dynamic EnergyPlus simulation.

Objectives

Technical objectives

  • Demonstrate an integrated passive-active retrofit workflow for a representative Irish dwelling.
  • Represent passive retrofit options and heat-pump design variables in one optimisation problem.
  • Quantify trade-offs between cost, emissions, annual heating demand, heat-pump electricity, seasonal COP and overheating risk.
  • Generate EnergyPlus-ready IDF cases for future dynamic simulation validation.
Methodology

Integrated passive-active screening workflow

The reduced-order model is used for fast screening; selected cases are exported for EnergyPlus validation.

Interpretation

What the pilot shows

The lowest-emissions pathway combines deep envelope upgrades, low-temperature emitters, improved ventilation and high-COP heat-pump operation, but it also raises an overheating-risk signal. The lowest-cost pathway is technically simple but leaves higher electricity use and poorer COP. This supports the fellowship argument that heat-pump readiness should be judged as a combined package problem, not only as an equipment replacement.

Next step

Route to fellowship-scale research

The next technical step is to replace the screening assumptions with calibrated Irish archetypes, simulate selected Pareto packages in EnergyPlus using Irish weather files, and train a physics-informed surrogate model for fast optimisation across multiple representative Irish dwelling typologies.
